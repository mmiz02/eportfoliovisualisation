Variation of corporate tax:
The visualization showed significant variation in corporate tax rates across industries. Some large tech companies and multinationals appeared to pay lower effective tax rates than smaller, more localized firms.

Companies paying less vs. high tax:
Industries such as technology and pharmaceuticals often paid less due to offshore subsidiaries and tax incentives, while retailers and utilities tended to pay higher effective rates because they operate mostly within domestic markets.

Reasons some companies pay less:

Tax loopholes and foreign income deferrals

Use of tax havens

R&D credits and government incentives

Interesting discoveries:

Even within the same industry, tax rates varied widely, reflecting corporate structure and accounting strategy.

The visualization effectively used color and layout to show not just values, but patterns of inequality in the tax system.

It demonstrated how data visualization can expose systemic differences in corporate behavior that raw numbers alone might hide.
